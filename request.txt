You are a programming consultant with electroplating expertise.

I want you to create a Python-based fast engineering approximation script to estimate the effect of a perforated insulating shield on electroplating thickness uniformity over a panel.

## Objective
Create a simulation that predicts relative plating thickness distribution (%) over a panel and estimates uniformity metrics, especially standard deviation across the panel, based on shield geometry and panel oscillation.

This is a feasibility tool, not a full FEM solver.

## Required modeling approach
Use a fast engineering approximation, not full electrostatic FEM.

Use this modeling concept:
1. Represent the cathode panel as a 2D grid.
2. Represent the shield as a perforated transmission mask.
3. Use a kernel-based field dispersion model:
   - Option C = open-area transmission + dispersion kernel
   - effective field = aperture mask convolved with a dispersion kernel
4. Simulate panel oscillation relative to the fixed shield over time.
5. Integrate local deposition over the plating time.
6. Output relative thickness map normalized to 100% average thickness.
7. Assume reactant supply is constant.
8. Ignore optimization for now; focus on simulation feasibility.
